**Quantum Moiré Heterostructures: Flat-Band Engineered Room-Temperature Superconductor / Supercapacitor Platform**

**Project Lead:** Dr. Elden Wayne Whalen III, ShD

### Overview
Exploring next-generation quantum materials through **generalized flat-band moiré engineering** in hybrid heterostructures. This repository documents research into Magic-Angle Twisted Bilayer Graphene (MATBG), carbon-doped systems, Transition Metal Dichalcogenide (TMD) moiré stacks (e.g., WSe₂/WS₂), and phonon-engineered architectures aimed at achieving practical **room-temperature (or near-room-temperature) quantum superconductivity combined with ultra-high capacitance storage**.

### Core Vision
By combining:
- **Flat-band physics** from twisted moiré superlattices
- **Engineered phonons** and crystal phonon lattice gates
- **Seebeck effect / phonon drag** for active thermal management and energy harvesting
- **Hybrid carbon + TMD heterostructures** with topological protection

We pursue a unified **quantum superconductor/supercapacitor** platform that operates at ambient or near-ambient conditions. This work builds on the groundbreaking tunability of MATBG while generalizing the underlying flat-band mechanisms that enable strong electron correlations, enhanced electron-phonon coupling, and exotic quantum states.

### Key Research Directions
- Optimization of moiré flat bands in graphene and TMD systems
- Phonon recycling and lattice gate architectures for thermal stabilization
- Carbon-doped heterostructures and hybrid graphene/TMD stacks
- Integration with topological/stochastic quantum chip designs
- Theoretical and simulation frameworks for pushing critical temperature (Tc) toward room temperature
- Hybrid device concepts merging lossless superconducting transport with quantum capacitance

### Current Status
Frontier theoretical and conceptual exploration (2026). No ambient-pressure room-temperature superconductor has been experimentally validated yet, but flat-band moiré systems represent one of the most promising platforms for discovering the required physics. This repo serves as a living hub for literature, simulations, design notes, and collaborative development.

### Why This Matters
Success in this domain could revolutionize energy transmission and storage, computing, quantum technologies, transportation, and beyond — delivering lossless electronics, ultra-efficient grids, and scalable room-temperature quantum hardware.
