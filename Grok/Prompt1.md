### Background on MATBG
Magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene involves stacking two graphene sheets at a precise "magic" twist angle of about **1.1°**. This creates a moiré superlattice with flat electronic bands near the Fermi level, leading to strongly correlated electron behavior. Key discoveries (around 2018 from MIT) include:

- Correlated insulating states at half-filling.
- Unconventional superconductivity with critical temperatures up to ~1.7 K (very low, but notable for a pure carbon system).

The superconductivity in MATBG is often described as unconventional (not purely phonon-mediated like traditional BCS theory), with possible contributions from electron-electron interactions, though electron-phonon coupling is enhanced and actively studied. Moiré phonons (vibrational modes tied to the superlattice) play roles in the physics, including soft modes and potential impacts on pairing.

Real-world MATBG operates at cryogenic temperatures. Claims of higher-temperature superconductivity in twisted graphene or related systems remain highly debated and unproven at scale for practical room-temperature use.

- In MATBG, moiré phonons and electron-phonon coupling are enhanced.
